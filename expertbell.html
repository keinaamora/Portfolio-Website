ExpertBell


ExpertBell was the project that I designed when I was studying the UX Immersion course with CareerFoundry. The purpose of the app is to “enable anyone, anywhere to instantly chat with an expert in virtually any field.” The users of the app are able to book an appointment and connect with a chosen expert on any topic.

Research

To start with this project, I first researched about the app’s competitors. There were not a lot of apps that provides the same service as the expert project. So, apps that provide experts for a more specific niche was also looked at. Through that, I was able to identify what needs to be in the app and what cannot be added to it.

Ideation

Because this app will be used by people who need help in solving their problems, their needs and goals dictated how the was designed. From the user personas, user flows were created which ultimately became the guide for the site map. However, the site map was changed after a Closed Card Sorting using OptimalSort.

Wireframe

Low-Fidelity Prototypes were sketched on paper.

Mid-Fidelity Prototypes were created on Balsamiq.

The first High-Fidelity Prototypes were created from an Adobe XD wireframe kit.

The second High-Fidelity prototypes were created using Proto.io

Design

ExpertBell was designed to line up with the principles of Unity, Balance, Hierarchy, Proportion, and Emphasis. As a native iOS app, ExpertBell also follows the iOS guidelines.

Accessibility was also kept in mind during the design process of the app. All text meets the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. However, the most important design addition of the app is the comment feature on the video call. People who are hard of hearing or deaf are able to video call an expert without worrying whether or not the expert can use sign language.

Prototype

Studying to become a UX Designer, I learned that nothing is perfect. It is not perfection that we should achieve but we should all work to become better and better. That attitude should reflect on apps, as well. In our society, people’s wants and needs change every day. It is a UX Designer’s job to make sure design iterations are done again and again, to keep up with society’s fast pace. It is only by researching more, applying changes to prototypes, and testing the new prototypes, that products continue to improve and satisfy users.
